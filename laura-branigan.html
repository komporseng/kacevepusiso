Biography

Laura Branigan was born in New York on July 3, 1952. Most well known for her early 1980s Platinum single “Gloria,” she is also remembered for her hit songs “Solitaire” and “How Am I Supposed to Live Without You.” In addition, she was featured on the soundtracks of Ghostbusters, Flashdance, and Baywatch. Her single “Gloria” earned her a Grammy nomination, though she ultimately lost the honor to fellow pop singer Melissa Manchester. Before recording her debut, self-titled solo album in 1982, she belonged to a band called Meadow and also worked as a backing vocalist for Leonard Cohen.
